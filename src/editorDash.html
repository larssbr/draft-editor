workzilla launches in Trondheim to target coworking market

A new company, workzilla, a technology company that enables coworking at kafees and resturants, launches 1 november in response to demand for frictionless work at cafees from the coworking sector.

workzilla will help customers to find and book a seat and table at a cafe. And to be certain that they get fast wifi and electricity all day.. workzilla is part of a growing trend to book a workspace at a cafe that gives them fast reliable wifi, electricity and an available spot when they arrive and already works with telenor, choice hotels, espresso house.

Lars Brusletto, CEO of workzilla explains: "My vision is to make it easy to use the cafees and resturant everywhere accesable as workspaces. ".

The new company offers frictionless work at cafees. workzilla works by simpely ordering your spot at a nearby cafe thru the app.

Based in Trondheim, workzilla will initially employ 2 staff and has plans to recruit 10 more over the next twelve months. The company also plans to expand all over every major town in europe.

The booming target market is estimated at more than 100 million euro per year.

Prior to launching the company's founder Lars Brusletto, CEO worked as a programmer on summer jobs. He has also travled the world. Studied marine cybernetics at NTNU and is currently doing his second master degree at NTNUs school of entreprenourship. .
